Transcending Boundaries: Kainchi Dham's Sacred Aura Embraces Tech Titans Zuckerberg and Jobs

  • Darren Elder
  • 4 Dec 2019
  • 12 Comments

Kainchi Dham, nestled in the serene Kumaon Hills of Uttarakhand, has emerged as a magnet for seekers of spiritual wisdom. This humble abode, consisting of lime-washed structures and a modest temple, holds profound significance for devotees, including renowned personalities like Mark Zuckerberg and Steve Jobs.

The story begins with Neem Karoli Baba, also known as Maharaj-ji, a revered Hindu guru and an ardent devotee of Hanuman, the Hindu deity. Maharaj-ji's spiritual influence extended beyond India, attracting numerous American seekers during the 1960s and 70s.

Legend has it that during a train journey, Maharaj-ji, then known as Baba Lakshman Das, encountered an incident that would forever impact the village of Neem Karoli. After being forced off the train at this village, he struck a deal with the railway officials. In return for allowing him back on board, he requested the construction of a railway station at Neem Karoli and improved treatment of sadhus (holy men). Astonishingly, once Maharaj-ji boarded the train, it resumed its journey, reinforcing his mystical aura. Maharaj-ji's intuitive abilities were awe-inspiring, often leaving individuals speechless as he effortlessly delved into their thoughts and memories. He possessed no material possessions and shunned worldly attachments, freely giving away any gifts he received. His nomadic lifestyle, constant muttering of "Ram, Ram, Ram," and unpredictable behavior added to his enigmatic persona.

Kainchi Dham, situated approximately 38 km from Nainital, the first temple was inaugurated in June 1964. This sacred ashram became a cherished place for Maharaj-ji's followers, including prominent spiritual teachers such as Ram Dass, Bhagavan Das, Krishna Das, and Jai Uttal. Notably, influential personalities like Steve Jobs, Mark Zuckerberg, Julia Roberts, Larry Page, and Jeffrey Skoll have also been deeply impacted by his teachings.

Interesting facts to know about Kainchi Dham Ashram:

  • Kainchi is a mountain ashram located in the Kumoan Hills in Uttarakhand, approximately 38 km from Nainital.

  • The first temple of Lord Hanuman was inaugurated in June 15, 1964.

  • In 1962, Maharajji (Baba Neem Karoli) built a chabootara (platform) around a place where two spiritual gurus Sadhu Premi Baba and Sombari Maharaj had performed Yagyas in Kainchi village.

  • The Hanuman temple was later built over the platform, thus establishing the Kainchi Ashram.

  • Baba Neem Karoli died on September 10, 1973. A temple for him was subsequently built at the Ashram. His statue was consecrated on June 15, 1976.

  • Neem Karoli Baba left his home in 1958 when his youngest daughter was 11 years old. He wandered throughout northern India as a sadhu and was known by many names.

  • Karoli Baba has temples in several places, including Lucknow and an Ashram in Texas, US. According to his website, Maharajji established at least 108 temples.

  • Anyone who wants to visit the Ashram in Kainchi village needs to request prior permission. After a reference from one of the older devotees, one can stay for three days. The ashram is closed for a large portion of the year as it becomes very cold there.

  • A lot of foreigners visit the Ashram. Spiritual leaders Ma Jaya, Ram Dass, musicians Jai Uttal and Krishna Das, humanitarian Larry Brilliant, scholar and writer Yvette Rosser, and Daniel Goleman are among his devotees.

  • Apple founder Steve Jobs visited the Ashram to meet Neem Karoli Baba with his friend Dan Kottke. However, he could not meet Baba who had died before his arrival.
